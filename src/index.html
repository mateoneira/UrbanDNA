MATEO NEIRA

{architecture | urbanism | spatial data science}

BIO:

Architect and spatial data researcher interested in understanding interactions of people and space, particularly the influence of urban morphology on mobility patterns and its effects on social segregation. As an MSc. Candidate at the Centre for Advanced Spatial Analysis at UCL, my dissertation focuses on the application of graph theory to transport systems (road network and public transport network) to explore relationships between network structure, land use and socio-economic variables; using different accessibility measure and random walks to build a spatial segregation index. As a former research assistant at llactaLAB-Sustainable cities research group at the university of Cuenca I worked on two main research projects, one related to active mobility patterns and the other related to the use of public space around river margins. My work mainly focused on using space syntax methods, exploratory data analysis techniques and quantitative methods to better understand the influence of the built environment on individual and collective behaviour.
